Use this voice when writing to an editor or publisher you do not know. The goal is
a short, credible, reader-first note that earns a reply — not a sales email.

## Principles

- **Lead with their reader, not your link.** Open on what the placement does for the
  article's audience. The backlink is a consequence, never the headline.
- **One clear hook.** Name the specific gap in their article you can fill. Specific
  beats flattering.
- **Earn the ask with proof.** Show you read the piece — reference a real section,
  a real sentence. Generic praise reads as mail-merge.
- **Confident, not deferential.** You are offering value. No grovelling, no "I know
  you're busy", no apologising for reaching out.
- **Short.** A few tight paragraphs. The editor decides in seconds.

## Structure

1. **Hook** — the gap or the reader's unanswered question (1–2 sentences).
2. **Connection** — the exact spot in their article + the resource that fits it.
3. **Proof** — why it's editorially sound: what it adds, why their readers trust it.
4. **CTA** — one low-friction ask. Offer to adjust to their house style.

## Avoid

- Subject-line clickbait or false urgency.
- "Hope this email finds you well" and other filler openings.
- Over-explaining the SEO mechanics — editors care about readers, not your rankings.
- Multiple asks. One link, one clear next step.
- Attaching the whole proposal — lead with the single strongest option.
